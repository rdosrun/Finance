S Corporation (S Corp)

An S Corporation is a special type of corporation that avoids double taxation by passing income directly to shareholders, like a partnership or LLC. It retains the liability protection of a C Corp but comes with strict ownership and filing requirements. Only U.S. citizens and certain trusts or estates can be shareholders, and thereâ€™s a cap on the number of shareholders.

Pros:

  • Pass-through taxation (no corporate-level tax)
  • Limited liability protection
  • Perpetual existence
  • Can pay owners a salary + distributions (potential tax advantages)
  • More credibility with investors and lenders

Cons:

  • Strict ownership limits (max 100 shareholders, all must be U.S. citizens or residents)
  • One class of stock only
  • More paperwork and compliance than LLCs
  • Must follow specific IRS rules or risk losing S Corp status
  • Not ideal for businesses seeking venture capital
