Sole Proprietorship

Sole proprietorship is the most common type of business. This kind of business is owned and operated almost exclusively by one individual. The major drawback of this kind of business is that it doesn’t provide any protection to the individual’s other assets such as real estate, retirement, etc., from liabilities such as debts.

Pros:

  • Easy and inexpensive to set up
  • Complete control and decision-making power
  • Owner receives all profits
  • Simple tax filing (income reported on personal tax return)
  • Less regulation and paperwork

Cons:

  • Unlimited personal liability for business debts
  • Harder to raise capital or get loans
  • Limited resources and skills (one-person operation)
  • Business ends if the owner retires, becomes incapacitated, or dies
  • Less credibility compared to corporations or LLCs
