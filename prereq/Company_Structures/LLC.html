Limited Liability Company (LLC)

A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a flexible business structure that blends features of both partnerships and corporations. It protects its owners (called members) from personal liability for business debts, while offering pass-through taxation like a sole proprietorship or partnership. LLCs can be owned by individuals, other companies, or even foreign entities. Theyâ€™re popular for their simplicity, liability protection, and tax flexibility.

Pros:

  • Limited liability for all members
  • Pass-through taxation by default (no double taxation)
  • Flexible management structure (can be member- or manager-managed)
  • Fewer formal requirements than corporations
  • Profits and losses can be distributed flexibly

Cons:

  • Can be more expensive to form than a sole proprietorship or partnership
  • Some states impose extra taxes or fees (e.g., franchise tax)
  • May require more paperwork than simpler structures
  • Limited lifespan in some states unless specified otherwise
