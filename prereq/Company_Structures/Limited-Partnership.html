Limited Partnership (LP)

A limited partnership consists of at least one general partner and one or more limited partners. The general partner manages the business and has unlimited liability, while limited partners contribute capital and have liability only up to their investment. Limited partners typically donâ€™t participate in day-to-day operations.

Pros:

  • Limited liability for limited partners
  • Ability to attract investors without giving up control
  • Pass-through taxation (no double taxation)
  • Flexible profit sharing options

Cons:

  • General partner has unlimited liability
  • More complex and costly to set up than a general partnership
  • Limited partners have little to no say in management
  • Can be harder to manage relationships between general and limited partners
  • Legal formalities and documentation required
